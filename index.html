Nutrition Basics for Everyone

Understanding nutrition doesn't have to be complicated. Here's what every Kenyan should know:

1. The Food Groups

Every meal should include foods from these groups:

  • Carbohydrates: Ugali, rice, potatoes, chapati
  • Proteins: Beans, lentils, eggs, chicken, fish
  • Vegetables: Sukuma wiki, spinach, carrots, tomatoes
  • Fruits: Bananas, oranges, mangoes, avocados

2. Portion Control

Use your hand to measure portions:

  • Protein: Palm size (beans, meat, fish)
  • Carbs: Fist size (ugali, rice)
  • Vegetables: Two fists (sukuma wiki, cabbage)
  • Fats: Thumb size (oil, avocado)

3. Hydration

Water is essential for health:

  • Drink 8 glasses of water daily
  • Carry water with you always
  • Limit sugary drinks
  • Eat water-rich foods (watermelon, oranges)

4. Meal Timing

When to eat matters:

  • Breakfast: Most important meal
  • Lunch: Should be your largest meal
  • Dinner: Light and early (before 8 PM)
  • Snacks: Fruits, nuts, yogurt between meals

Disease Management

Nutrition solutions for common health issues in Kenya

Managing Common Diseases with Nutrition

Proper diet can help manage and even prevent many chronic diseases

Diabetes Management

Goal: Maintain stable blood sugar levels

Foods to Eat:

  • Whole grains (brown rice, whole wheat ugali)
  • Beans and lentils (high fiber)
  • Non-starchy vegetables (sukuma wiki, cabbage)
  • Lean proteins (fish, chicken, eggs)
  • Healthy fats (avocado, nuts, olive oil)
Get Diabetes Plan

Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)

Goal: Lower blood pressure naturally

Foods to Eat:

  • Fresh vegetables (especially sukuma wiki - high potassium)
  • Bananas (potassium helps balance sodium)
  • Garlic and onions (natural vasodilators)
  • Watermelon (contains citrulline)
  • Oats and whole grains
Get Hypertension Plan

Obesity/Weight Management

Goal: Healthy, sustainable weight loss

Recommended:

  • Small frequent meals (5-6 small meals daily)
  • Drink water before meals (reduces appetite)
  • Increase vegetables (fill up on low-calorie foods)
  • Walk 30 minutes daily (start with 10 minutes)
  • Protein with every meal (keeps you full longer)
